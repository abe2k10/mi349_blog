Balancing Work and College Life

September 25, 2025 by Abe Mohamednoor

Being both a college student at Michigan State University and an Amazon Safety Representative has taught me how important time management really is. At first, I thought I could simply “wing it” and handle both responsibilities without planning. That did not last long. I quickly realized that without a clear schedule, I would fall behind on assignments or miss important updates at work. Learning to balance these two big parts of my life has been a challenge, but also a rewarding experience. Every day is a chance to grow, and I’ve learned to appreciate the value of structure.

One strategy I use is blocking out time for both school and work tasks. For example, I reserve mornings before my shift to review lecture notes or prepare for upcoming assignments. Even if it’s only thirty minutes, the consistency makes a difference. At work, I carry a small notebook to jot down safety observations or things I want to follow up on later. This helps me stay present in the moment while still preparing myself for schoolwork when I get home.

Of course, balance isn’t always perfect. Some weeks are harder than others, especially around exams or busy work seasons. But I’ve noticed that when I prioritize sleep, communicate with my managers and professors, and stay organized, I can handle the pressure. This balancing act isn’t easy, but it has made me more disciplined, more thoughtful, and better prepared for the future.

Why Safety Matters Everywhere

September 24, 2025 by Abe Mohamednoor

Working in safety at Amazon has changed the way I look at the world. Before I started this job, safety was something I only thought about when an accident happened. Now, I see it everywhere: in the way people drive, how they lift boxes, and even how my classmates set up their desks. Safety is not about avoiding trouble; it is about creating habits that keep everyone healthy and able to keep doing what they love.

One lesson I have learned is that small details matter. Wearing gloves, putting on safety glasses, or taking a few extra seconds to check equipment can prevent serious injuries. These actions may feel small in the moment, but they add up over time. I’ve seen how much smoother a workplace runs when people take safety seriously from the start. It builds trust among coworkers and creates a culture where everyone looks out for each other.

I also think about safety at home and school now. Something as simple as keeping walkways clear or adjusting your chair so your back doesn’t hurt can make a big difference. To me, safety is not just a rule—it’s a way of respecting yourself and others. That’s why I’m passionate about sharing this message with classmates, coworkers, and even family. The more we treat safety as part of daily life, the stronger and healthier our communities will be.
